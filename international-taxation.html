International Taxation New

Cross-border tax structuring for businesses with global ambition.

Operating, hiring or raising capital across more than one country adds a layer of tax exposure that's easy to underestimate. We help you structure for it deliberately, rather than discovering the gaps during an audit.

Cross-border structuring Part of our established finance practice
What We Cover

The areas that matter once a business crosses a border.

Cross-border tax exposure rarely shows up in one place — it's spread across how you're structured, how you price intercompany transactions, and how consistently you file.

Tax Treaties

Applying double-taxation treaties correctly so income isn't taxed twice across jurisdictions, and identifying where treaty relief actually applies to your structure.

Transfer Pricing

Documenting and defending intercompany pricing between related entities in different countries, in line with local and OECD-aligned requirements.

Entity Structuring

Choosing and setting up the right entity type and jurisdiction mix for how you actually plan to operate, hire and repatriate funds — not a one-size template.

Compliance & Filing

Keeping filings current across the jurisdictions you operate in, with a calendar built so deadlines don't surprise you at year-end.

Who This Is For

Built for businesses that have already crossed a border — or are about to.

Businesses expanding across jurisdictionsOpening a second-country entity, hiring abroad, or setting up a subsidiary for the first time.
NRI and foreign-investor taxIndividuals and entities with cross-border income, holdings or investment exposure into or out of India.
Import / export structuringBusinesses whose trade flows create tax exposure on both sides of a transaction, and need that exposure planned for in advance.
